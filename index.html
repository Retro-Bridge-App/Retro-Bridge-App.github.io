What is Retro Bridge?

RetroBridge is a cross-chain asset-transfer app; Alchemy's profile describes Retro Bridge as a Web3 bridge that streamlines asset transfers across blockchains. The official destination is retrobridge.io, whose page title is All chains, single app.

How Retro Bridge works

  1. Open the verified domainUse the official app link and confirm the browser shows retrobridge.io before connecting a wallet.
  2. Connect a walletConnect the wallet that holds the source-chain asset and keep enough native gas for approvals and transfer transactions.
  3. Choose the routeSelect the asset, source network, destination network, amount, and receiving wallet, then compare the route details shown in the app.
  4. Review and signCheck fees, timing, chain IDs, approvals, and wallet prompts before signing; never share a seed phrase.

Before you bridge

Official app route

The primary action opens retrobridge.io so visitors can continue in the live app after checking the domain.

Multi-chain focus

The Alchemy listing shows Ethereum, Arbitrum, Avalanche, Base, BNB Chain, Polygon, OP Mainnet, Starknet, ZKsync and Blast among the app's networks.

Risk-aware flow

Ethereum.org notes that cross-chain transfers can carry smart-contract, technology, operator, and user-error risks; check route details and wallet prompts before signing.

Retro Bridge FAQ

What is retro bridge?

Retro Bridge is the search name for RetroBridge, a cross-chain asset-transfer app listed by Alchemy as a Web3 bridge for streamlining transfers across blockchains.

What is the official retro bridge URL?

The official app URL is https://retrobridge.io/; verify the retrobridge.io domain in your browser before connecting a wallet.

Which networks does retro bridge support?

Alchemy's listing shows networks including Ethereum, Arbitrum, Avalanche, Base, BNB Chain, Polygon, OP Mainnet, Starknet, ZKsync and Blast; always confirm live route availability inside the app.

Do I need a wallet for retro bridge?

Yes. A wallet connection and wallet signatures are required for the transfer flow, along with enough native gas on the source network.

Is retro bridge risk free?

No. Cross-chain transfers can involve smart-contract, technology, liquidity, routing, operator, and user-error risk; ethereum.org's bridge overview is a useful primer.
