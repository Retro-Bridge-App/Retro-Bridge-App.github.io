Why use Retro Bridge

A direct DeFi route without giving up custody

Wallet-native from start to finish

You connect a Web3 wallet, review the transaction and sign it yourself. Retro Bridge does not require deposits into a centralized account.

Built around real bridge intent

Start with Ethereum assets, choose the receive side and let the app prepare the bridge swap path for the connected network.

Cost and timing visible before approval

Bridge swaps depend on liquidity, chain conditions and gas costs, so Retro Bridge keeps the live quote and route review inside the app before confirmation.

FAQ

Retro Bridge questions, answered

01

What is Retro Bridge?

Retro Bridge is a non-custodial DeFi app for bridge-style swaps. It helps you move from ETH into USDC while keeping transaction approval in your own wallet.

02

Which chains and assets does it support?

The page preview highlights ETH to USDC. Availability can vary by route, liquidity and network, so check the current list on the supported chains page and inside the app.

03

How long does a Retro Bridge swap take?

Timing depends on source chain confirmation, destination chain activity and route execution. The live estimate is shown in the app before you sign.

04

What does Retro Bridge cost?

Network fees and route pricing change with market and chain conditions. Retro Bridge shows the live quote, token output and network cost before confirmation.

05

Is Retro Bridge custodial?

No. Retro Bridge is designed for self-custody: you connect a wallet, review the route and approve the transaction from that wallet.

06

How should I check a route?

Confirm the token, chain, receiving asset, allowance and wallet prompt. For broader DeFi market context, compare liquidity and network conditions before sending larger transactions.
